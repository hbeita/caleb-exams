📝 Regular Verbs in Past Tense Done►

Regular verbs follow rules. Learn the 4 rules!

Rule 1: Ends in E

Just add -d

live - livedlove - loved

Rule 2: Consonant + Y

Change Y to I, add -ed

try - triedstudy - studied

Rule 3: Vowel + Y

Keep the Y, add -ed

play - playedenjoy - enjoyed

Rule 4: Other verbs

Just add -ed

walk - walkedclean - cleaned
✅ Affirmative and Negative Sentences Done►

Affirmative

Subject + past verb + complement

"I ate pizza last night."

"She watched a movie yesterday."

"They played soccer after school."

Negative

Subject + didn't + base verb

"I didn't eat pizza last night."

"She didn't watch a movie yesterday."

"They didn't play soccer after school."

Key rule: After "didn't", always use the BASE form of the verb — NOT the past form!
Correct: She didn't finish. Wrong: She didn't finished.
🔢 Countable and Noncountable Nouns Done►

Countable (C)

You can count them one by one.

apple - 1 apple, 2 apples pencil, lemon, pineapple student, chair, book

Noncountable (N)

You cannot count them. No "s" form.

water (not "2 waters") sugar, salt, bread sand, air, milk
📊 Quantity Words: too, much, many, enough Done►
TOO

More than needed (negative meaning)

"The pasta is too hot to eat."

MUCH

Used with NONCOUNTABLE nouns

"My coffee has much sugar."

MANY

Used with COUNTABLE nouns

"I have many apples at home."

ENOUGH

The right amount you need

"I don't have enough money."
